# ⚡ Verbal Ability Shortcuts & Grammar Cheatsheet

A quick reference guide for essential grammar rules, sentence structure, and vocabulary-solving tricks for placement verbal rounds.

---

## 1. Golden Rules of Subject-Verb Agreement
A singular subject takes a singular verb, and a plural subject takes a plural verb.
- **Rule of 'And':** If two singular nouns are joined by *and*, they generally take a plural verb.
  *Example:* Ram and Shyam **are** playing.
  *Exception:* If they represent a single idea, they take a singular verb: *Bread and butter **is** my favorite breakfast.*
- **Rule of 'Either/Or' and 'Neither/Nor':** The verb agrees with the subject closest to it.
  *Example:* Neither the teacher nor the **students were** present.
  *Example:* Neither the students nor the **teacher was** present.
- **Rule of 'As well as', 'Along with', 'Together with':** The verb agrees with the first subject.
  *Example:* The **captain**, along with his team members, **was** praised. (Captain is singular)
- **Each, Every, Everyone, Someone, Nobody:** Always take a singular verb.
  *Example:* Each of the boys **has** (not *have*) submitted the assignment.

## 2. Active & Passive Voice Conversions
Keep in mind that the tense **does not change** in active-passive conversions (unlike direct-indirect speech).

| Tense | Active Voice | Passive Voice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Present** | He writes a letter. | A letter **is written** by him. |
| **Present Cont.** | He is writing a letter. | A letter **is being written** by him. |
| **Simple Past** | He wrote a letter. | A letter **was written** by him. |
| **Past Cont.** | He was writing a letter. | A letter **was being written** by him. |
| **Present Perfect** | He has written a letter. | A letter **has been written** by him. |
| **Simple Future** | He will write a letter. | A letter **will be written** by him. |

---

## 3. Direct & Indirect Speech Rules
Unlike voice, the tense **changes** back in time (if the reporting verb is in the past tense).

- **Tense Shifts:**
  - Simple Present $\rightarrow$ Simple Past
  - Present Continuous $\rightarrow$ Past Continuous
  - Present Perfect $\rightarrow$ Past Perfect
  - Simple Past $\rightarrow$ Past Perfect
  - Will / Shall $\rightarrow$ Would / Should
  - Can / May $\rightarrow$ Could / Might
- **Time/Place Shifts:**
  - Today $\rightarrow$ That day
  - Yesterday $\rightarrow$ The day before / The previous day
  - Tomorrow $\rightarrow$ The next day / The following day
  - Now $\rightarrow$ Then
  - Here $\rightarrow$ There
  - This $\rightarrow$ That

## 4. Error Spotting Tricks
- **Look for Parallelism:** Make sure all parts of a list have the same grammatical form.
  *Incorrect:* He likes swimming, hiking, and **to run**.
  *Correct:* He likes swimming, hiking, and **running**.
- **Its vs. It's:**
  - **Its:** Possessive pronoun (e.g., *The dog wagged its tail*).
  - **It's:** Contraction of *it is* or *it has* (e.g., *It's raining*).
- **Preposition Errors:** Look for incorrect usage of prepositions with certain verbs:
  - Discuss/Describe $\rightarrow$ Do not use *about* after them (e.g., *We discussed the project*, not *We discussed about the project*).
  - Comprise $\rightarrow$ Do not use *of* after it (e.g., *The book comprises ten chapters*, not *comprises of*).
- **Although / Though:** Must be followed by *yet* or a comma, never by *but*.
  *Incorrect:* Although he worked hard, **but** he failed.
  *Correct:* Although he worked hard, **yet** he failed. / Although he worked hard, he failed.
