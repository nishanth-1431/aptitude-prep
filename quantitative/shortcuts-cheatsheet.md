# ⚡ Quantitative Aptitude Shortcuts Cheatsheet

A quick reference guide for essential formulas, tricks, and methods to solve Quantitative Aptitude questions rapidly.

---

## 1. Fractions and Percentages Conversion
Convert fractions to percentages instantly to speed up calculations:
- $1/2 = 50\%$
- $1/3 = 33.33\%$
- $1/4 = 25\%$
- $1/5 = 20\%$
- $1/6 = 16.66\%$
- $1/7 = 14.28\%$
- $1/8 = 12.5\%$
- $1/9 = 11.11\%$
- $1/10 = 10\%$
- $1/11 = 9.09\%$
- $1/12 = 8.33\%$

## 2. Speed, Time, and Distance (TSD)
- **Km/hr to m/s conversion:** Multiply by $\frac{5}{18}$ (e.g., $72 \text{ km/h} = 72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 20 \text{ m/s}$)
- **m/s to Km/hr conversion:** Multiply by $\frac{18}{5}$
- **Average Speed (when distance is constant):**
  $$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2xy}{x+y}$$ (where $x$ and $y$ are two different speeds)
- **Relative Speed:**
  - Same Direction: $S_1 - S_2$
  - Opposite Direction: $S_1 + S_2$
- **Boats and Streams:**
  - Let Speed of Boat in still water $= u$, Speed of Stream $= v$
  - Downstream Speed ($d$) $= u + v$
  - Upstream Speed ($u_s$) $= u - v$
  - Speed of Boat in still water $u = \frac{d + u_s}{2}$
  - Speed of Stream $v = \frac{d - u_s}{2}$

## 3. Profit, Loss, and Discount
- **Gain %** $= \left(\frac{\text{Gain}}{\text{Cost Price}}\right) \times 100$
- **Loss %** $= \left(\frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{Cost Price}}\right) \times 100$
- **Selling Price (SP)** $= \left(\frac{100 + \text{Gain}\%}{100}\right) \times \text{Cost Price}$
- **Selling Price (SP)** $= \left(\frac{100 - \text{Loss}\%}{100}\right) \times \text{Cost Price}$
- **Discount:** Marked Price (MP) $-$ Selling Price (SP)
- **Discount %** $= \left(\frac{\text{Discount}}{\text{Marked Price}}\right) \times 100$
- **Successive Discounts:** Two successive discounts of $a\%$ and $b\%$ is equivalent to a single discount of:
  $$\text{Effective Discount} = \left(a + b - \frac{ab}{100}\right)\%$$

## 4. Simple and Compound Interest
- **Simple Interest (SI):**
  $$\text{SI} = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$$
- **Compound Interest (CI):**
  $$\text{Amount (A)} = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T$$
  $$\text{CI} = A - P$$
- **CI for Half-yearly compounding:**
  $$\text{Amount (A)} = P \left(1 + \frac{R/2}{100}\right)^{2T}$$
- **Difference between CI and SI:**
  - For 2 years: $\text{Diff} = P \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$
  - For 3 years: $\text{Diff} = P \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2 \left(3 + \frac{R}{100}\right)$

## 5. Time and Work
- If A can do a piece of work in $x$ days, A's 1 day work $= 1/x$.
- If A can do a work in $x$ days and B can do it in $y$ days, then working together they can finish it in:
  $$\text{Days} = \frac{xy}{x+y}$$
- **MDH Formula (for chain rule):**
  $$\frac{M_1 \times D_1 \times H_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 \times D_2 \times H_2}{W_2}$$
  *(where $M = \text{Men}, D = \text{Days}, H = \text{Hours/day}, W = \text{Work done/Wages}$)*
- **Pipes and Cisterns:**
  - Inlet Pipe (fills): Positive work ($+1/x$)
  - Outlet/Leak Pipe (empties): Negative work ($-1/y$)
